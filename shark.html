The Great White Shark

The great white shark also known as the great white, white pointer,

white shark, or white death,

is a species of large lamniform shark which can be found in the coastal surface waters of all the major oceans.

The great white shark is mainly known for its size,

with the largest individuals known to have approached or exceeded

20 ft in length, and 5,000 lb in weight.

This shark reaches its maturity around 15 years of age

and can have a life span of over 30 years.
